Curriculum-ready

Clear learning modules mapped to Y10–11 objectives: properties, chemical behaviour, equations and exam technique.

Rich Animations

Interactive reaction simulations (Na + H₂O, Li + O₂, K flame, Na + Cl₂) using SVG/canvas + GSAP for memory-friendly visualisation.

Practice & Assessment

Why animations?

Visual motion and cause-effect (e.g., a sodium piece fizzing and producing hydrogen) make it easier for students to remember observations and link them to balanced equations.

Quick facts

  • Valence electrons: 1
  • Typical ion: M⁺
  • Reactivity: increases down the group
  • Hydroxides: strong alkalis (MOH)
