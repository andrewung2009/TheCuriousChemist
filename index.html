What are Alkanes?

Alkanes are saturated hydrocarbons. They contain only single covalent bonds between carbon atoms. This means each carbon is bonded to as many hydrogen atoms as possible.

General Formula

General formula for alkanes: CₙH₂ₙ₊₂

Example: Butane → n = 4 → C₄H₁₀

Properties & Uses

  • Physical: Non-polar, insoluble in water, boiling point increases with chain length.
  • Chemical: Combust to form CO₂ + H₂O; react with halogens under UV light.
  • Uses: Methane for cooking gas; Butane/Propane for Liquefied Petroleum Gas (LPG); Octane in petrol.
