📚 What Are Alkanes?

  • Alkanes are saturated hydrocarbons — they contain only single covalent bonds.
  • Each carbon forms 4 single bonds, creating chains or branches.

🧮 General Formula

CnH2n+2

Example: n = 4 → C₄H₁₀ (butane)

🧪 Properties & Uses

Physical

  • Non-polar → insoluble in water
  • Higher BP with longer chain

Chemical

  • Combust → CO₂ & H₂O
  • Substitution with halogens

Uses

  • Methane: cooking gas
  • Butane/propane: LPG
  • Octane: petrol
