📚 What Are Alkanes?

  • Alkanes are saturated hydrocarbons — only single covalent bonds.
  • Each carbon makes 4 single bonds, forming chains or branches.

🧮 General Formula

CnH2n+2

Example: n = 4 → C₄H₁₀ (butane)

🧪 Properties & Uses

Physical

  • Non-polar → insoluble in water
  • Boiling point ↑ with longer chain

Chemical

  • Combust → CO₂ & H₂O
  • Substitution with halogens

Uses

  • Methane: cooking gas
  • Butane/propane: Liquefied Petroleum Gas (LPG)
  • Octane: petrol

🔍 True or False?

⚡ Common Misconceptions

  • Alkanes are unsaturated ❌ (They are saturated!)
  • Alkanes dissolve in water ❌ (They are non-polar!)
  • Combustion gives only carbon monoxide ❌ (It gives CO₂ and H₂O!)
  • All hydrocarbons are alkanes ❌ (No! Alkenes, alkynes exist too!)
