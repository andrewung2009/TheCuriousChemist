📚 What Are Alkanes?

  • Alkanes are saturated hydrocarbons — only single covalent bonds.
  • Each carbon makes 4 single bonds, forming chains or branches.

🧮 General Formula

CnH2n+2

Example: n = 4 → C₄H₁₀ (butane)

🧪 Properties & Uses

Physical

  • Non-polar → insoluble in water
  • Boiling point ↑ with longer chains

Chemical

  • Combusts to CO₂ and H₂O
  • Substitution with halogens (UV light)

Uses

  • Methane: cooking gas
  • Butane/Propane: LPG
  • Octane: petrol

⚡ Common Misconceptions

  • ❌ Alkanes are unsaturated → (They are saturated!)
  • ❌ Alkanes dissolve in water → (They are non-polar!)
  • ❌ Combustion gives only CO → (It gives CO₂ + H₂O!)
  • ❌ All hydrocarbons are alkanes → (No! Alkenes & alkynes too!)
