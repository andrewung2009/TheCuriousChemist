📚 What Are Alkanes?

  • Alkanes are saturated hydrocarbons — only single covalent bonds.
  • Each carbon forms 4 single bonds, creating chains or branches.

🧮 General Formula

CnH2n+2

Example: n = 4 → C₄H₁₀ (butane)

🧪 Properties & Uses

Physical

  • Non-polar → insoluble in water
  • Boiling point increases with chain length

Chemical

  • Combusts to carbon dioxide and water
  • Undergoes substitution with halogens under ultraviolet light

Uses

  • Methane: cooking gas
  • Butane/Propane: liquefied petroleum gas (LPG)
  • Octane: petrol

⚡ Common Misconceptions

  • ❌ Alkanes are unsaturated → They are saturated!
  • ❌ Alkanes dissolve in water → They are non-polar!
  • ❌ Combustion only gives carbon monoxide → It gives carbon dioxide and water!
  • ❌ All hydrocarbons are alkanes → There are alkenes and alkynes too!
