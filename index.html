Alkanes

  • Alkanes are saturated hydrocarbons.
  • Saturated → contain only single covalent bonds between carbon atoms.
  • Hydrocarbon → made of carbon and hydrogen only.

Each carbon in alkanes sits at the centre of a tetrahedron with bond angles of 109.5 °.

CnH2n+2

Example: n = 4 → C₄H₁₀ (butane)

1️⃣ Combustion

Complete:
CH₄ + 2 O₂ → CO₂ + 2 H₂O

Incomplete:
CH₄ + O₂ → CO + H₂O

2️⃣ Substitution Reaction

Chlorination (UV light):
CH₄ + Cl₂ → CH₃Cl + HCl

Properties
  • Non-polar → insoluble in water
  • Boiling point ↑ with chain length
  • Combustion → CO₂ + H₂O
Uses
  • Cooking gas: propane, butane
  • Petrol: octane
  • Paraffin wax
