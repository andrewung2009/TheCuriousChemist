📚 What Are Alkanes?

  • Alkanes are saturated hydrocarbons.
  • Saturated → only single covalent bonds.
  • Hydrocarbon → carbon & hydrogen only.

🔺 Tetrahedral Geometry

Each carbon sits at the centre of a tetrahedron with bond angles of 109.5°.

🧮 General Formula

CnH2n+2

Example: n = 4 → C₄H₁₀ (butane)

⚙️ Common Reactions

1️⃣ Combustion

Complete: CH₄ + 2 O₂ → CO₂ + 2 H₂O

Incomplete: CH₄ + O₂ → CO + H₂O

2️⃣ Substitution

CH₄ + Cl₂ → CH₃Cl + HCl (UV light)

🧪 Properties & Uses

Properties
  • Non-polar → insoluble in water
  • Boiling point ↑ with chain length
  • Combustion → CO₂ + H₂O
Uses
  • Cooking gas: propane, butane
  • Petrol: octane
  • Paraffin wax
